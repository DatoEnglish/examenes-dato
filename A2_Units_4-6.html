Reading Passage

Today is Friday, and Daniel is making plans for the weekend. On Saturday morning, he is meeting his friend Sofia at a café. He likes Sofia because she always helps him with his English homework, and she makes him feel calm and confident. Daniel is excited because he is going to surprise her with a small gift.

Before meeting Sofia, Daniel is going to a shop near his house. He is going to buy her a bouquet of flowers and a simple piece of jewelry. He feels a little nervous, but also very happy. “I hope she likes them,” he thinks. The flowers are beautiful, and the jewelry is not expensive, but it is special for her.

After breakfast, Daniel and Sofia are walking in the park. Daniel gives her the gifts, and she smiles when she sees them. She feels surprised and thankful, and she tells him that the flowers are lovely. This moment makes them both feel relaxed and joyful.

In the afternoon, Daniel is visiting his parents. He usually calls them every day, but today he wants to see them in person. His mom looks tired, so Daniel says he is going to help her in the kitchen. His dad shows him old photos, and they all feel peaceful together.

On Sunday, Daniel is going to stay at home and rest. He isn’t making many plans because he wants to relax and enjoy his time. “These weekends help me feel ready for the new week,” he says with a smile.

Reading Passage

Last year was very important for Emma. She finished college and got her first job in a department store. She worked as a salesperson, and she helped customers every day. Emma liked her job, but she didn’t earn a lot of money at first, so she needed to be careful with her expenses

During her first months at work, Emma learned how to save money. She didn’t spend too much on clothes, and she didn’t waste money on things she didn’t need. Sometimes, she shopped online, but only when there was a sale. She saved part of her salary every month and paid back the money she borrowed from her parents.

One of Emma’s big goals was to buy a car. At first, she didn’t have enough money, so she borrowed some from her parents. After a few months, she finally bought a small car. She felt proud because she worked hard and made good decisions.

Later that year, Emma met her future husband, Alex. They didn’t plan to get married quickly, but they fell in love. In the end, they got married in a small ceremony with family and friends. After everything that happened, Emma asked herself, “Did I make good decisions this year?” She smiled and thought, “Yes, I did.”
